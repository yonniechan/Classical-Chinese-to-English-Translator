Tsze-chang asked, 'What must the officer be, who may be said to be distinguished?' The Master said, 'What is it you call being distinguished?' Tsze-chang replied, 'It is to be heard of through the State, to be heard of throughout his clan.' The Master said, 'That is notoriety, not distinction. 'Now the man of distinction is solid and straightforward, and loves righteousness. He examines people's words, and looks at their countenances. He is anxious to humble himself to others.
